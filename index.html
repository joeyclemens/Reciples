Mild Dan Dan
Noodles

A family-friendly take on the Sichuan classic — rich sesame-peanut sauce, springy noodles, and a savoury tofu topping. Heat dialled back for little ones, chilli oil on the side for adults.

⏱ Under 30 minutes 🌱 Vegetarian 👶 Toddler-friendly
4

◆ Ingredients

◆ Method

1
Make the sauce

Whisk together the sesame paste or peanut butter, tahini, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, warm water, and sugar until completely smooth. Taste and adjust — it should be nutty, savoury, and slightly tangy. Set aside.

2
Cook the noodles

Cook the noodles according to packet instructions. In the last minute, add the pak choi or spinach to the boiling water to wilt. Drain everything together and toss with a little sesame oil to stop sticking.

⏱ ~5 min
3
Fry the tofu

Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Add the crumbled firm tofu and fry for 5–6 minutes, breaking it up as it cooks. Add garlic and ginger and fry for another minute. Season with a splash of soy sauce.

⏱ ~7 min
4
Assemble

Divide noodles and greens into bowls. Spoon the sauce over generously and toss to coat. Top with the tofu mixture, sliced spring onions, and toasted sesame seeds. Serve chilli oil on the side for the adults.

◆ Tips & Notes

Toddler tip: The sauce as written is mild, but taste before serving. If using shop-bought peanut butter, some brands are quite rich — thin with extra warm water for a smaller portion.
Noodle swap: Udon works really well here and tends to go down a treat with kids. Spaghetti in a pinch is totally fine too.
Make it quicker: The sauce takes 2 minutes to make. If you prep it while the noodles boil, the whole thing comes in well under 25 minutes.
Storage: The sauce keeps in the fridge for 3–4 days. Make a double batch and use it as a dipping sauce or cold noodle dressing mid-week.
